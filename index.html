About Me

Profile of Sive Dumbisa South African Afro-soul singer:

  • Artist Name : Sive Dumbisa
  • Style of Music : Afro soul and pop
  • Production sytle : Jazz, afro-soul and R&B

Sive Dumbisa is quickly becoming a consummate artist possesing great skill, command and fair on stage. Coming from humbled begginings in Butterworth, he has ascended to perform on major platforms, combining country soulfull sounds and jazz elements with the mainstream. Sive`s extensive and impressive resume is the product of hard work and the sort of excellence that bodes well for a young artist with an exciting career ahead.

South African Afro-soul singer,songwriter and self taught guitarist from Eastern Cape Butterworth. "I want to inspire set your soul alive and be the voice of God in the lives of many through my Music" He is a Social conscious and solution advocate that inspires,spreads love and a remedy to the broken hearted through his music.
Singing at the age of 7years at home and in Sunday school. He was influenced by the soulful country music and worship songs in church, which is the main reasons why he does music that speaks and has native flavour. Eventually became a professional artist after he was discovered at the Buffalo City carnival talent search,During his varsity years and eversince never looked back.

He is a newly recording artist produce by the legendary and SAMA award winning Nduduzo Makathini. Already making noise and loved by many in numerous Corporate and outdoor events around the country prior his album release.

He has shared a stage with many house hold names in South Africa in events and festivals such as Summer season carnival East London and King (2013) , East London beach festival (2013), National arts festival  Dakawa stage (2014), Daily dispatch Christmas party(2015), East London summer season light festival (2015), University of Forthare centenary celebration with minister of social development Bathabile Dlamini (woman in politics) 2016,Eastern Cape department of arts and culture retirement farewell of senior officials (2016), Freedom fighters memorial lecturer at East London town hall (2016), Eastern Cape Talent search finals as guest artist, Grahamstown arts festival Dakawa stage (2016), Durban music Imbizo festival  (2016), Moshito music festival Johannesburg radio park (2016), Macufe Festival (2016),Motsepe Foundation music fesival east london(2016) recently state of the province adress(2017), Eastern Cape youth month Celebration (2017).

He was involved in the Nelson Mandela institute project aimed at preserving mother tongue and helping with early child development, in which with other artists he re-arranged and recorded songs with kids for the purpose of classroom learning.
